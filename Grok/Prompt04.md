**WordPress.org + WooCommerce (self-hosted) has no monthly platform fee like Shopify, making it potentially cheaper long-term, but total costs depend on hosting, plugins, maintenance, and your technical setup.** For a knife store (pocket knives, kitchen knives, swords, rifle scopes), expect **$50–$300+/month** realistically for a functional operation, or **$600–$3,600+ per year** for small-to-medium scale. This is often lower than Shopify at higher volumes but requires more hands-on management.

### Core Cost Breakdown (2026 Estimates)
- **WooCommerce Plugin**: Free (open-source core).

- **Web Hosting** (biggest ongoing cost): 
  - Shared/basic: $5–$20/month (promotional; renews higher, e.g., $15–$45).
  - Managed WordPress/WooCommerce-optimized (recommended for performance with large inventory): **$15–$100+/month** (e.g., SiteGround, Bluehost, Cloudways, Pressable, Kinsta, or Rocket.net).
  - Scales with traffic, product count (large inventory needs good resources), and features like caching/SSL. Rural UP Michigan location doesn't directly affect hosting costs.

- **Domain Name**: **$10–$20/year** (typically $12–$25 renewal for .com). Often free first year with hosting.

- **Theme**: Free options exist; premium WooCommerce-compatible themes: **$50–$150 one-time** (or $20–$100/year for updates/support).

- **Plugins/Extensions**: Essential for inventory management, shipping (heavy items like swords), SEO, security, backups, compliance. 
  - Many free; paid ones add **$100–$800+/year** total (e.g., for advanced shipping, abandoned carts, reviews).

- **Payment Processing**: No platform cut from WooCommerce. Typical rates ~2.9% + $0.30 per transaction (Stripe, etc.), but **knives/swords/weapons often face restrictions**. Mainstream options like WooPayments/Stripe/PayPal may limit or prohibit blade sales, requiring specialized "knife-friendly" or high-risk merchant accounts (potentially higher fees or setup costs).

- **Other Ongoing**:
  - Security/backups/SSL: Often in hosting; premium ~$50–$200/year.
  - Maintenance/Updates: DIY free (but time-intensive) or hire help ($50–$300+/month).
  - Email marketing, analytics, etc.: Variable.

**Realistic Totals**:
- **Starter/low-volume**: $200–$1,000/year (cheap hosting, minimal plugins, DIY).
- **Typical small-medium knife store** (large inventory, good performance): **$1,000–$3,000+/year** (~$80–$250/month).
- Higher if you hire developers for custom work, advanced features, or high traffic.

### Advantages vs. Shopify for Your Use Case
- **Lower fixed costs** at scale (no $29–$399/month subscription).
- More flexibility for custom inventory/shipping rules and compliance with restricted items.
- Full data ownership and no platform restrictions on product types (though payment processors still apply).

**Disadvantages**: Requires more technical know-how (or hiring help) for security, updates, speed optimization, and backups. Downtime or poor performance hurts sales more directly than on Shopify. Large inventory means you'll need solid hosting and inventory plugins.

### Recommendations for Your UP Michigan Knife/Scopes Store
Start with managed WooCommerce hosting (e.g., SiteGround or similar) for ease. Budget extra for specialized payment processing due to blades/scopes. Test with a small subset of inventory. Factor in your time or developer costs for setup (can be $500–$5,000+ one-time if not DIY). 

WooCommerce can be very cost-effective with your existing large inventory, but success still hinges on marketing, SEO, and operations more than the platform fee. Compare total cost of ownership (TCO) as your sales grow—many find it cheaper than Shopify long-term for niche retail. Check current hosting promos and verify payment gateway compatibility for weapons-related items upfront. Consult a web developer or accountant for your specifics.
