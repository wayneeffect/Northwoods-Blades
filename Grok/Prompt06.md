### Hourly Rates for Mid-Level WooCommerce/WordPress Developers (2026)
- **US / North America mid-level**: $45–$100/hr, with strong consensus around **$60–$85/hr** for e-commerce work (higher end for WooCommerce customizations, integrations, and performance tuning).
- Specialized WooCommerce skills (custom plugins, shipping rules for heavy/bladed items, inventory management for large catalogs, compliance features) often push rates toward **$70–$100+/hr**.
- **AI advantage**: Tools help you generate code, themes, plugins, and content faster, letting you deliver in fewer billable hours while maintaining quality and charging competitive fixed prices.

**Target $65–$85/hr** if you're US-based (or serving US clients from Michigan's UP), have a portfolio of live stores, and can demonstrate AI-accelerated workflows for speed and cost savings to clients.

### Project-Based Pricing (Recommended)
Clients often prefer fixed quotes. For a knife/outdoor gear store (large inventory import, product variants/filters for knives/scopes/swords, custom shipping, basic compliance notes, SEO setup, security/performance optimization):

- **Basic setup** (premium theme, product import, core WooCommerce config, essentials): **$2,500–$5,000**.
- **Mid-level professional store** (customizations, plugins for inventory/shipping/reviews, integrations, branding, optimization, launch support): **$4,000–$9,000** (most common range for mid-level developers).
- **Advanced** (heavy custom work, complex filtering, API integrations, migrations): **$8,000–$15,000+**.

**AI boost** allows you to complete projects faster (e.g., what took 50–80 hours manually might take 30–50 hours), improving your margins.

### Separate Website (If Needed)
WooCommerce *is* a full website (built on WordPress), so it includes pages, blog, and content marketing features—no separate site is usually required.

- If the client wants a distinct informational/marketing site (e.g., for heavy SEO/content separate from the store): Add **$1,500–$5,000** (or hourly).
- Many bundle a basic blog/content setup into the main WooCommerce project.

### Factors That Influence Pricing
- **Scope** — Large inventory means more time on organization, variants, and bulk uploads. Knives/scopes add needs for custom shipping, restrictions, and filters.
- **Your value-add** — Niche experience (outdoor gear), AI efficiency, post-launch maintenance retainer ($300–$1,500/month), training, and documentation.
- **Location** — US clients pay premium; your lower UP Michigan cost of living lets you remain competitive while profitable.
- **Deliverables** — Hosting recommendations, security setup, performance optimization (critical for WooCommerce), and 1–2 rounds of revisions.

**Tips for Pricing & Selling Services**:
- Use tiered packages (Basic / Professional / Premium) for easier sales.
- Emphasize WooCommerce benefits: lower long-term platform costs, full ownership, flexibility for restricted items like blades.
- Require 40–50% upfront, with clear milestones.
- Document assumptions (e.g., client provides product data) to avoid scope creep.
- Build a portfolio with similar e-commerce sites; highlight AI-driven fast delivery.
